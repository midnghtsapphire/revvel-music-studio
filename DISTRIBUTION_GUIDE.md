## 1. How Music Distribution Works

Digital music distribution is the process of delivering your music to Digital Service Providers (DSPs) — the streaming platforms and download stores where listeners consume music. The major DSPs include Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, YouTube Music, Tidal, Deezer, Pandora, iHeartRadio, TikTok/Instagram (for short-form content), and Shazam.

The distribution chain works as follows:

> **Artist** → **Distributor (Aggregator)** → **DSPs (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.)** → **Listeners**

Distributors are the intermediaries that handle the technical delivery of your audio files, metadata, and artwork to every platform simultaneously. They also collect your royalties from each platform and pay you on a regular schedule (usually monthly or quarterly).

---

## 2. Can You Distribute Music Yourself?

The short answer is **no, not directly**. Spotify, Apple Music, and virtually all major DSPs do not accept music submissions directly from individual artists. They work exclusively through licensed distributors who have established technical integrations and contractual relationships with each platform. This system ensures quality control, proper metadata formatting, and reliable royalty accounting.

Therefore, you **must** use a distributor (also called an aggregator) to get your music onto these platforms. The good news is that modern distributors make this process straightforward and affordable, with some offering free tiers.

## 4. Step-by-Step Distribution Process

### Step 1: Prepare Your Audio

Export your final master as a high-quality audio file. The industry standard requirements are:

- **Format:** WAV (preferred) or FLAC
- **Sample Rate:** 44.1 kHz minimum; 96 kHz recommended for highest quality
- **Bit Depth:** 16-bit minimum; 24-bit recommended
- **Channels:** Stereo
- **Loudness:** Target -14 LUFS for Spotify, -16 LUFS for Apple Music

Revvel Music Studio handles all of this automatically — the `pipeline` command cleans, masters, and exports at 96kHz/24-bit stereo.

### Step 2: Prepare Your Artwork

- **Dimensions:** Minimum 3000 x 3000 pixels (some platforms require 3000 x 3000 exactly)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Color Space:** RGB
- **Content Rules:** No URLs, social media handles, phone numbers, pricing, or copyrighted images

### Step 3: Gather Your Metadata

Before uploading, have the following information ready:

- **Song title** (exactly as you want it displayed)
- **Artist name** (Revvel)
- **Album/single title**
- **Genre and subgenre** (e.g., Alt Pop, Indie Pop, K-pop Fusion)
- **Release date** (schedule at least 4-6 weeks ahead for playlist consideration)
- **Songwriter credits** (legal names of all writers)
- **Producer credits**
- **Language** of the lyrics
- **Explicit content** flag (yes/no)
- **Copyright holder** (HOTRS - House of the Rising Sun)
- **Copyright year**

### Step 4: Upload to Your Distributor

1. Log in to your distributor's dashboard
2. Click "New Release" or equivalent
3. Upload your audio file(s)
4. Upload your artwork
5. Fill in all metadata fields
6. The distributor will automatically generate ISRC codes for each track and a UPC code for the release
7. Review everything carefully
8. Submit

### Step 5: Wait for Delivery

After submission, your distributor delivers your release to all DSPs. Typical delivery times:

| Platform | Delivery Time |
|---|---|
| Spotify | 2-5 business days |
| Apple Music | 1-3 business days |
| Amazon Music | 2-5 business days |
| YouTube Music | 3-5 business days |
| TikTok/Instagram | 1-7 business days |
| All platforms | Up to 2-3 weeks for full global delivery |

### Step 6: Promote

Once live, claim your artist profiles on Spotify for Artists and Apple Music for Artists. Submit future releases for playlist consideration at least 7 days before release.

## 5. ISRC Codes, UPC Codes, and Metadata

### ISRC (International Standard Recording Code)

An ISRC is a unique 12-character code assigned to each individual recording. It follows the format: `CC-XXX-YY-NNNNN` where CC is the country code, XXX is the registrant code, YY is the year, and NNNNN is a unique designation. Your distributor typically generates these for you automatically. If you want to manage your own ISRCs, you can register as an ISRC Manager through your national ISRC agency (in the US, this is RIAA).

### UPC (Universal Product Code)

A UPC is a barcode assigned to each release (single, EP, or album). Your distributor generates these automatically.

### Why Metadata Matters

Accurate metadata ensures you get paid correctly, your music appears in the right searches, and listeners can find you. Always double-check spelling, capitalization, and credits before submitting.

## 6. Copyright Registration and Protection

Your music is automatically copyrighted the moment you create and record it — this is called **common law copyright**. However, for full legal protection, you should formally register your works.

### How to Register Copyright (United States)

1. Visit [copyright.gov](https://www.copyright.gov/)
2. Create an account on the Electronic Copyright Office (eCO)
3. Select "Register a Work"
4. Choose work type: **Sound Recording** (for the recording) and/or **Musical Work** (for the composition)
5. Fill in the application with title, author(s), claimant(s), and dates
6. Upload a deposit copy of your audio file
7. Pay the fee: approximately **$45-$65** per work (single author, online filing)
8. Processing takes 3-6 months

### Benefits of Registration

- Creates a public record of your ownership
- Required to file an infringement lawsuit in federal court
- Enables recovery of statutory damages and attorney's fees
- Creates a legal presumption of validity

### Batch Registration

For your catalog of 295 songs, you can register multiple works in a single application if they share the same author(s) and were unpublished, or if they were published as a collection. This can significantly reduce costs.

## 7. Performance Rights Organizations (PROs)

As a songwriter and publisher, you earn **performance royalties** every time your music is played publicly — on streaming platforms, radio, TV, in stores, at live venues, and more. To collect these royalties, you must register with a Performance Rights Organization.

### Major PROs

| PRO | Website | Joining Fee | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **BMI** | [bmi.com](https://www.bmi.com/) | Free | Songwriters (free to join) |
| **ASCAP** | [ascap.com](https://www.ascap.com/) | $50 one-time | Songwriters and publishers |
| **SESAC** | [sesac.com](https://www.sesac.com/) | Invitation only | Established artists |

### What to Do

1. **Join as a Songwriter:** Register yourself as a songwriter/composer with BMI or ASCAP
2. **Join as a Publisher:** Register HOTRS (House of the Rising Sun) as a publishing entity with the same PRO
3. **Register Your Works:** Add each of your songs to your PRO's catalog, including all writer and publisher splits
4. **Collect Royalties:** Your PRO will track performances and pay you quarterly

## 8. Royalty Types and How You Get Paid

As a recording artist, songwriter, and label owner, you are entitled to multiple streams of royalties:

| Royalty Type | Who Pays | Collected By | Typical Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Streaming Royalties** | DSPs (Spotify, etc.) | Your distributor | $0.003-$0.005 per stream |
| **Performance Royalties** | Radio, TV, venues | Your PRO (BMI/ASCAP) | Varies by usage |
| **Mechanical Royalties** | Streaming services | Harry Fox Agency / MLC | $0.0009 per stream (approx.) |
| **Sync Licensing** | Film, TV, ads | Sync agent or direct | $500-$50,000+ per placement |
| **YouTube Content ID** | YouTube | Your distributor | Varies |

### The Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC)

Register at [themlc.com](https://www.themlc.com/) to collect mechanical royalties from streaming services in the US. This is free and separate from your PRO registration.

## 12. Using Revvel Music Studio for Distribution

Revvel Music Studio automates the technical preparation of your music for distribution.

### Quick Distribution Workflow

```bash
# Step 1: Clean up your raw recording
revvel cleanup my_song.wav -o my_song_clean.wav

# Step 2: Master it (choose a preset that matches your genre)
revvel master my_song_clean.wav --preset warm -o my_song_master.wav

# Step 3: Generate a video for YouTube/social media
revvel video my_song_master.wav --title "My Song" --type visualizer

# Step 4: Prepare distribution package
revvel distribute my_song_master.wav \
  --title "My Song" \
  --artist "Revvel" \
  --label "HOTRS - House of the Rising Sun" \
  --distributor ditto \
  --genre "Alt Pop"

# Or do everything in one command:
revvel pipeline my_song.wav --preset cinematic --title "My Song"
```

### API Workflow

```python
import requests

# Authenticate
token = requests.post("http://localhost:8000/auth/token", json={
    "username": "revvel", "password": "hotrs2026"
}).json()["access_token"]

headers = {"Authorization": f"Bearer {token}"}

# Full pipeline
with open("my_song.wav", "rb") as f:
    resp = requests.post(
        "http://localhost:8000/pipeline/full",
        headers=headers,
        files={"audio": f},
        data={
            "title": "My Song",
            "artist": "Revvel",
            "mastering_preset": "warm",
            "create_video_flag": True,
        }
    )
print(resp.json())
```
